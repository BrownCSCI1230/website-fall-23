Realtime Pipeline

Rasterization

What does rasterization do? How does rasterization fit into the realtime pipeline in terms of inputs and outputs?

Answer:

Rasterization is the process of determining which pixels should be covered based on the input vertex data. In the OpenGL pipeline, rasterization occurs between the vertex and fragment shaders. In GLSL terminology, the vertex shader output gl_Position is first clipped using the space and the resultant truncated geometries are used to check for covered pixels. These covered pixels are called "fragments" and are then processed in the fragment shader.

glVertexAttribPointer(1, 3, GL_FLOAT, GL_FALSE, 6 * sizeof(GLfloat), reinterpret_cast<void *>(3 * sizeof(GLfloat)));

How many vertices and how many triangles are described in the VBO/VAO pair?

Answer:

vertices and triangle.

Explanation:

In this case we see that each vertex is of size elements by looking at 6 * sizeof(GLfloat). Then given there are 18 elements, there are vertices. Each triangle is made out of triangles so there is triangle described by this VAO.
